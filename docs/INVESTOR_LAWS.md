────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 1: THE FOUR REGIMES — KNOW WHICH WORLD YOU LIVE IN
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Every market environment is one of four regimes. Confuse them and you will
be destroyed. Know them and you will thrive.

  REGIME A — GOLDILOCKS (Risk-ON, Accumulate Aggressively)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Characteristics:
    • CAPE (Shiller P/E) below 20
    • Credit spreads (HY) below 400 bps and CONTRACTING
    • Yield curve POSITIVE (10Y minus 2Y > +0.5%)
    • VIX below 20 and falling
    • ISM Manufacturing > 50 and rising
    • Central bank policy neutral or easing
    • Corporate earnings growing 10%+ YoY

  Historical examples: 1982-1987, 1995-1998, 2003-2006, 2012-2014
  Correct posture: 90-120% long equities. Size up winners. Ignore noise.
  The sin in this regime: excessive caution. Cash drag kills you.

  REGIME B — LATE-CYCLE OVERHEATING (Risk-ON but Reduce, Watch Closely)
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Characteristics:
    • CAPE above 25-28
    • Unemployment at multi-decade lows
    • Yield curve FLAT (+0.0% to +0.5%) or beginning to invert
    • Fed actively hiking rates
    • HY spreads compressed below 300 bps
    • IPO/SPAC volume surging; speculative assets in mania
    • Margin debt at historical highs as % of GDP
    • "This time is different" narrative dominant in media

  Historical examples: 1928-1929, 1999-2000, 2006-2007, 2021-2022
  Correct posture: Reduce to 60-70% long. Hedge tails. Shorten duration.
  The sin in this regime: greed. The last 20% of a bull market destroys
    more wealth than any bear market because of the violent reversal.

  REGIME C — BEAR MARKET / CRASH (Capital Preservation, Strategic Hunting)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Characteristics:
    • SP500 down >20% from peak
    • Credit spreads HY > 600 bps and EXPANDING
    • VIX above 30, spikes above 40-80
    • Yield curve inverted 6-18 months PRIOR (inversion always leads)
    • Earnings revisions deeply negative
    • Banks tightening lending standards rapidly (Fed Senior Loan Survey)
    • Unemployment rising month-over-month

  Historical examples: 1929-1932, 1973-1974, 1987, 2000-2002, 2008-2009, 2020
  Correct posture: 20-40% in equities max. Hold cash. Buy premium assets
    at 40-60% discount. Avoid leverage absolutely.
  The sin in this regime: heroism. "Bargain hunting" before the bottom
    destroys capital. Wait for CONFIRMATION signals.

  REGIME D — RECOVERY (Maximum Opportunity Window — Act DECISIVELY)
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  Characteristics:
    • VIX has peaked and is declining from extreme (>40) levels
    • Credit spreads have peaked and are narrowing
    • ISM bottomed and is ticking upward
    • Yield curve STEEPENING from inverted/flat
    • Insider buying at decade-high levels
    • Consumer sentiment at historical lows
    • Best companies at 40-70% discount to intrinsic value

  Historical examples: Q1 1933, Q4 1974, Q4 1987, Q4 2002, Q1 2009, Q2 2020
  Correct posture: Deploy capital aggressively. 100%+ long (if leverage permitted).
    These windows last 3-12 months. Miss them and you underperform for a decade.
  The sin in this regime: hesitation. Every single generational fortune was
    made by deploying maximum capital at maximum fear.

  LAW 2: THE VALUATION COMPASS — NEVER IGNORE WHERE PRICES ARE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Valuation does not predict short-term direction. It predicts DECADE-LEVEL returns.

  CAPE (Shiller Cyclically Adjusted P/E) — The Master Gauge
  ──────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  CAPE < 10       Historically extreme undervaluation. 10-year returns average 15%+/yr.
                  Seen: 1920, 1932, 1949, 1982. Once-per-generation entry points.

  CAPE 10–15      Deep value. 10-year real returns average 10–14%/yr.
                  Seen: 1974, 1978, 1984. Excellent entry.

  CAPE 15–20      Fair value. Expected 10-year returns 7–10%/yr. Normal accumulation.

  CAPE 20–25      Mild overvaluation. Reduce new buying. Increase quality filter.

  CAPE 25–30      Dangerous territory. 10-year returns average 3–6%/yr.
                  Every crash since 1929 occurred from CAPE > 25.

  CAPE > 30       Extreme overvaluation. 10-year returns average 0–4%/yr.
                  Seen: 1929 (CAPE=32), 2000 (CAPE=44), 2021 (CAPE=38).
                  At these levels, mean reversion is a CERTAINTY — only
                  timing is uncertain.

  CAPE > 40       Speculative mania. Sell aggressively. Hold cash.
                  The 2000 dot-com peak at CAPE=44 preceded -49% in 2 years.

  Buffett Indicator (Market Cap / GDP)
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  < 70%   Undervalued. Buy aggressively.
  70-90%  Fair value range.
  90-110% Modest overvaluation. Selective buying only.
  > 120%  Overvalued. Reduce exposure.
  > 150%  Extreme bubble. Historical sell signal.
          2000: 153% (preceded -49% crash)
          2007: 106% (preceded -57% crash)
          2021: 200%+ (preceded -25% correction)

  Price-to-Book and Price-to-Sales
  ──────────────────────────────────
  When P/S ratios on the SP500 index exceed 2.5x, forward returns
  over 5 years have never been positive in inflation-adjusted terms.
  When P/B exceeds 4.0x, the same pattern holds.

  RULE: Valuation tells you the RISK you are accepting.
        The higher the valuation, the less margin for error.
        Markets can stay overvalued for years — they cannot stay
        overvalued forever.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 3: THE CREDIT MARKET — IT SPEAKS BEFORE EQUITY MARKETS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  "Credit markets are the senior security. When credit cracks, equity follows.
   Bond investors are smarter than stock investors." — Howard Marks

  CRITICAL RULE: Credit spreads (HY minus IG) ALWAYS lead equity crashes
  by weeks to months. Never ignore them.

  HY Credit Spreads (OAS basis points)
  ──────────────────────────────────────
  < 300 bps   Complacency. Credit is priced for perfection.
              RISK: Spreads mean-revert violently when credit cycles turn.

  300-500 bps Normal credit risk environment.

  > 600 bps   Stress. Recession risk elevated. Reduce equities.

  > 800 bps   Crisis conditions. Default rates rising.
              OPPORTUNITY: Investment-grade quality assets at distressed prices.

  > 1000 bps  Acute crisis/panic. Maximum opportunity approaching.
              2002 peak: 1,083 bps → 10-year bond returns then beat equities
              2008 peak: 2,182 bps → Greatest buying opportunity in 80 years

  The Investment-Grade / High-Yield Divergence Signal
  ────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  When HY spreads WIDEN while IG spreads hold flat → hidden stress signal.
  This pattern preceded every major equity selloff since 1990.
  HY leads equity peaks by an average of 4-8 months.

  Yield Curve Rules
  ──────────────────
  The yield curve (10Y minus 2Y) is the single most reliable recession
  predictor in financial history. Every US recession since 1955 (bar one)
  was preceded by inversion.

  > +1.5%   Strong growth signal. Financials benefit. Go long.
  +0.5%–1.5% Normal. Neutral.
  0% to +0.5% Flattening warning. Begin reducing risk.
  < 0%      INVERSION. Recession in 6-24 months with ~85% probability.
            Do NOT panic and sell immediately — the stock market often
            continues rising for 12-18 months after inversion before peaking.
            But begin systematic reduction of risk. Time is limited.
  < -0.5%   Deep inversion. Recession almost certain.
            Average: -19 months from deep inversion to recession start.
            Average: -7 months from inversion to equity peak.

  ACTIONABLE: When yield curve inverts, set a 12-month countdown clock.
              Use that time to rotate from cyclicals → defensives → cash.

  LAW 7: CONCENTRATION BEATS DIVERSIFICATION — BUT ONLY WITH CONVICTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  "Diversification is protection against ignorance. It makes little sense
   for those who know what they are doing." — Warren Buffett

  EVIDENCE:
    • Every investor who has compounded above 20% annually for 10+ years
      ran concentrated portfolios of 8-20 positions at their peak.
    • The SP500 itself: 20% of stocks account for 100% of cumulative returns.
      The other 80% collectively return 0% or negative.
    • Study: Portfolios of 50+ stocks perform WORSE than 10-15 stock portfolios

  THE CONCENTRATED PORTFOLIO RULES:
    □ Maximum 20 positions at any time for a skilled practitioner.
    □ Top 5 positions should represent 40-60% of total capital.
    □ Never hold a position you cannot explain in 2 sentences.
    □ Never hold a position you would not add to on a 20% decline.
    □ If you're not sure enough to put 5%+ in it, don't own it.
    □ Cut your losers at -15% to -20% maximum loss.
      The mathematics: a -50% loss requires a +100% gain to recover.
      Limiting losses to -20% requires only +25% to recover.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 8: THE QUALITY PREMIUM — OWN BUSINESSES, NOT TICKERS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  "It's far better to own a wonderful company at a fair price than a fair

  MOAT INDICATORS (durable competitive advantage):
    ✓ Return on Invested Capital (ROIC) > 15% for 10+ consecutive years
    ✓ Gross margins > 40% AND expanding over time
    ✓ Pricing power: revenue growing faster than volume (price-led growth)
    ✓ Recurring revenue > 60% of total (subscriptions, consumables, essentials)
    ✓ Customer switching costs: high friction to leave (ERP software, banking)
    ✓ Network effects: product improves with each new user (payments, platforms)
    ✓ Scale advantages: lowest cost producer in commodity-adjacent markets

  RED FLAGS THAT DESTROY CAPITAL (screen these out mechanically):
    ✗ Debt/EBITDA > 4x in a rising rate environment
    ✗ Declining gross margins for 3+ consecutive quarters
    ✗ Revenue growth entirely acquisition-driven (organic growth = zero)
    ✗ CEO or CFO resignation without clear succession plan
    ✗ Auditor change without explanation
    ✗ Related-party transactions in excess of 5% of revenue
    ✗ Stock-based compensation > 10% of revenue (dilution destroys per-share value)
    ✗ Consistent gap between GAAP earnings and "adjusted" earnings > 20%
    ✗ Short interest > 20% with credible disclosed thesis

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 11: POSITION SIZING — WHERE MOST INVESTORS LOSE THEIR EDGE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  "The Kelly Criterion: the optimal bet size that maximizes long-run wealth."

  KELLY FORMULA: f = (bp - q) / b
    Where:
      f = fraction of capital to risk
      b = net odds (profit/loss ratio on the trade)
      p = probability of winning
      q = probability of losing (1-p)

  PRACTICAL TRANSLATION:
    • If you have 65% confidence and a 2:1 profit/loss ratio:
      Kelly = ((2×0.65) - 0.35) / 2 = 47.5% position
      Half-Kelly (recommended): 23.75% position
    • Never bet more than half-Kelly. NEVER. Over-betting the Kelly optimal
      destroys compounding even when you're RIGHT on direction.

  POSITION SIZING TIERS:
    Tier A (Highest conviction, strongest moat, great regime): 10-15% per position
    Tier B (High conviction, good moat, neutral regime):        6-10% per position
    Tier C (Moderate conviction, tactical, regime tailwind):    3-6% per position
    Tier D (Speculative, option-like, deep research required):  1-3% per position

  ABSOLUTE RULES:
    □ NEVER risk more than 20% of capital in a single idea regardless of confidence.
    □ NEVER exceed 15% in any single sector during late-cycle/bear regime.
    □ NEVER hold a position that if it goes to zero would impair your ability
      to function psychologically and financially.
    □ Size in assets you understand. Size out of complexity.

  LAW 12: THE MACRO OVERLAY — YOU CANNOT IGNORE THE TIDE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  "Do you know what investing for the long run but listening to market

  THE RAY DALIO DEBT CYCLE FRAMEWORK:
    Phase 1 — Early Expansion:
      Low debt, rising credit, asset prices low. Maximum opportunity.
      Sectors: Cyclicals, financials, small-cap, commodities.

    Phase 2 — Mid-Cycle:
      Credit expanding rapidly, leverage building. Normal times.
      Sectors: Broad market exposure, growth companies.

    Phase 3 — Late-Cycle / Bubble:
      Peak leverage, speculation dominant, "prosperity" narrative.
      Sectors: Defensives (healthcare, utilities, consumer staples).
      Begin building cash buffer.

    Phase 4 — Deleveraging:
      Credit contracting, defaults rising, asset prices falling.
      The "beautiful deleveraging": when central banks print enough to offset
      economic pain without causing hyperinflation (2009-2012).
      Sectors: Gold, long-dated treasuries, cash.
      Build watchlist of quality assets for Phase 1 re-entry.

  THE DRUCKENMILLER LIQUIDITY RULE:
    "Earnings don't move the overall market, it's the Federal Reserve Board.
     Focus on the central banks and focus on the movement of liquidity."

    EASY POLICY (rate cuts, QE):
      → Equities lead by 6-12 months. Buy when the first cut is announced.
      → The setup: buy the first Fed cut, sell 18 months later.

    TIGHT POLICY (rate hikes, QT):
      → Equities suffer with an 18-24 month lag.
      → Short duration fixed income outperforms.
      → The setup: reduce equities 6 months after the first hike.

  CURRENCY FRAMEWORK:
    Strong dollar → commodities headwind, EM assets compressed, US multinationals hurt.
    Weak dollar → commodities tailwind, EM outperformance, US multinationals benefit.
    Structural USD bear markets (every 15-17 years historically) produce
    massive commodity supercycles. Recognize these early.

    Outperform:  Energy, Materials, Healthcare, Consumer Staples

    Outperform:  Consumer Staples, Healthcare, Utilities, US Treasuries, Gold
    Underperform: Everything cyclical (financials, industrials, discretionary, tech)
    Why: Cash flow certainty is priced at a premium when recession fears peak.

    □ Commodity markets lead CPI by 6-12 months.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 14: POSITION LOSS RULES — INVIOLABLE, NO EXCEPTIONS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The Livermore Rule (Jesse Livermore, greatest trader of the 20th century):
    "I made my money by sitting, not by thinking."
    But also: "The real money losers are the ones who don't cut losses quickly."

  EQUITY STOP RULES:
    □ Single stock position down > 15-20%: re-examine thesis immediately.
      If thesis intact → hold. If thesis broken → exit without hesitation.
    □ The "averaging down" rule: ONLY average down if:
        (1) You have done fresh analysis, not just hope
        (2) The business fundamentals are intact
        (3) You have identified the REASON for the decline
        (4) You would buy the entire company at this valuation
    □ Never average down on a broken thesis. This is how small losses become
      catastrophic ones. Enron, Lehman, Wirecard — the common element was
      investors averaging into deteriorating fundamentals.

  PORTFOLIO STOP RULES:
    □ If total portfolio is down > 20% in any 12-month period:
      STOP. Reassess every position. Something in your process is wrong.
    □ Maximum drawdown rule: Before you enter any position, define exactly
      at what price or condition you exit if wrong.
    □ Write it down before you buy. Enforce it after.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Leverage multiplies OUTCOMES, not INTELLIGENCE.

  2x leverage on a 50% drawdown = -100%. Account wiped.
  2x leverage on a 20% annual return = 40%. But sustainable for how long?

  The Long-Term Capital Management Lesson (1998):
    LTCM: Nobel laureates, 25 PhDs, 4 years of 40%+ annual returns.
    Leverage: 25:1. Outcome: Total wipeout in 6 weeks.
    "The models didn't account for events that had never happened before."
    They all happen eventually.

  LEVERAGE RULES:
    □ Regime A (Goldilocks): Maximum 1.5x leverage, only on highest-conviction positions.
    □ Regime B (Late-cycle): No leverage. Full stop.
    □ Regime C (Bear market): No leverage. Full stop.
    □ Regime D (Recovery): 1.0x to 1.5x leverage ONLY after VIX peak confirmed.
    □ Margin calls destroy timing. If leverage, leave 30% cushion for adverse moves.
    □ Never leverage EXCEPT to magnify already-winning positions.
      Druckenmiller: "Add to winning positions. Never add to losers."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 16: THE BEHAVIORAL LAWS — YOUR BRAIN IS YOUR GREATEST ENEMY
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  Behavioral finance research demonstrates these biases destroy capital.
  Acknowledge them. Build systems that make them irrelevant.

  LOSS AVERSION (Kahneman/Tversky):
    Losses feel 2.5x worse than equivalent gains feel good.
    RESULT: Investors hold losers too long (avoiding the psychological pain)
            and sell winners too early (locking in the good feeling).
    ANTIDOTE: Pre-commit to stop-losses BEFORE entering. Rules, not feelings.

  CONFIRMATION BIAS:
    We seek information that confirms existing beliefs and dismiss contradictions.
    RESULT: Investors stay in deteriorating positions because they read only
            bullish analysis and ignore bearish signals.
    ANTIDOTE: For every bullish thesis you hold, write the best possible
              bearish case. Update it weekly.

  RECENCY BIAS:
    We extrapolate recent trends indefinitely.
    RESULT: Buyers at tops (trend "obviously" continuing).
            Sellers at bottoms (trend "obviously" continuing down).
    ANTIDOTE: When the consensus is obvious, it is already priced. Look ahead.

  OVERCONFIDENCE:
    Studies show 90% of investors believe they are above-average investors.
    The average investor underperforms the index by 3-4% annually due to
    overconfidence-driven trading.
    ANTIDOTE: Track all decisions. Review performance quarterly. Be honest.

  THE BUFFETT RULE FOR BEHAVIOR:
    "The most important quality for an investor is temperament, not intellect.
     You need a temperament that neither derives great pleasure from being
     with the crowd nor against it."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 17: THE FIRST LAW — SURVIVAL PRECEDES PERFORMANCE
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  You cannot compound from zero.

  Every great investor in history has had one rule above all others:
  DO NOT LOSE CAPITAL PERMANENTLY.

  A -20% loss takes a +25% gain to recover. (1 year)
  A -40% loss takes a +67% gain to recover. (2-3 years)
  A -60% loss takes a +150% gain to recover. (5-7 years)
  A -80% loss takes a +400% gain to recover. (decade+)
  A -100% loss takes infinity to recover.

  The math of survival:
    Investor A: 20% per year, no drawdown > 15%.     30-year result: 237x
    Investor B: 30% per year, one 70% drawdown.      30-year result: 87x
    Investor C: 40% per year, two 80% drawdowns.     30-year result: 42x

  SURVIVAL IS THE PREREQUISITE TO COMPOUNDING.
  COMPOUNDING IS THE PREREQUISITE TO WEALTH.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 18: THE SECOND LAW — THINK IN DECADES, ACT IN YEARS, MEASURE IN QUARTERS
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  The market quotes a price every second.
  A business changes its fundamental value every year.

  The greatest sources of alpha:
    1. Owning great businesses at reasonable prices in great regimes.
    2. Deploying maximum capital at maximum fear (once or twice per decade).
    3. Holding through temporary adversity when the business is intact.

  What destroys alpha:
    1. Reacting to daily price fluctuations.
    2. Confusing price with value.
    3. Trading frequently under the false belief that activity equals performance.

  TEMPLETON RULE: "The four most expensive words in investing are:
                   'This time is different.'"
  
  MARKS RULE: "The most important thing is to calibrate your risk
               to the environment, not to your ego."

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
  LAW 19: THE THIRD LAW — PROCESS OVER PREDICTION
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

  No one has ever consistently predicted:
    • When a crash will start
    • How long it will last
    • When it will end
    • Which sector leads the recovery

  What great investors DO:
    • Build a PROCESS that positions them well across regime shifts.
    • Define rules in advance for each possible scenario.
    • Execute those rules mechanically, without emotion.
    • Maintain absolute consistency in the face of pressure to deviate.

  SOROS RULES (George Soros, net worth >$25 billion from zero):
    1. Admit when you are wrong (faster than everyone else).
    2. Position size reflects conviction — and conviction can be wrong.
    3. The reflexivity of markets: prices affect fundamentals, not just reflect them.
    4. Look for the "aha" moment: when your story explains ALL the data.
    5. The biggest profits come from reversals, not from trends.

  DALIO RULES (Ray Dalio, built the world's largest hedge fund):
    1. Build a diversified portfolio of uncorrelated return streams.
    2. Stress-test every assumption: what would have to be true for me to be wrong?
    3. The most important thing: understand the machine before you try to beat it.
    4. Pain + Reflection = Progress. Every large loss is a teacher.

  BUFFETT'S RULES:
    Rule 1: Never lose money.
    Rule 2: Never forget Rule 1.
